Community Engagement


We get questioned often about what the 'nature and culture of Bearspaw' is. This becomes clearest when we reference a resident of Bearspaw who went to grade school in the community forty years ago. At that time, there was only one non-farm family west of Bearspaw road. She asked us, "Do you mean now, or 40 years ago, or 10 years ago?". The inference was clearly that Bearspaw life has changed and it continues to do so. So, what is the nature and culture of Bearspaw?


Over the past five years, we have been interacting with residents across Bearspaw. It started with open houses and visits with our Willow Creek project. John moved into Bearspaw in July 2008 and he and his family have since gotten to know the neighbours and their thoughts on Bearspaw. John also sat for a year on the Bearspaw Technical Workshop where 30 panel members representing farmers, short and long-term residents and the development community participated in helping to develop the (now cancelled) Community Development Strategy (CDS). With the CDS, John also participated in the workshops, coffee houses and open houses that the county held. During this time the Ten Peaks team was attentive and listened to what the underlying tone and important factors were to those who lived in Bearspaw.

Engaging Residents and Garnering Feedback

Fundamentally, as Bearspaw, we all realize we have something special out here. We know that we are different than Cochrane and that we don't want to look like Tuscany or Rocky Ridge by the Co-opâ€¦or basically anywhere in Rocky View. Bearspaw is unique, and we all have a slightly different view based on how long we have lived here, where we live in Bearspaw or the size of our lots.

Most recently, we have visited with over 150 people face to face to see where they came in on eleven statements that make up the fundamental positions for Bearspaw residents (you can reference these statements on the For the Community questionnaire). With a great deal of commonality, Bearspaw residents agree that we want:

  1. 1. To have a vibrant, distinct and distinguished community.
  2. 2. A higher standard of architectural quality in our homes.
  3. 3. 'Dark sky' principles to protect the night skies, to see the stars.
  4. 4. Economic, social and environmental sustainability.
  5. 5. Protect or enhance vegetation, habitat and waterscapes.
  6. 6. Development to attend to water, waste-water and storm water needs.
  7. 7. New development to add to the value of our property.
  8. 8. Safety and security for our home and community.

As we continue to engage with the community we will be sharing results and feedback on our website. We look forward to your participation in our survey! Other comments and feedback are welcome at community@tenpeakscapital.com.
