History


Perhaps the best place to start is with an introduction to Ten Peaks. There are three principal owners of the Ten Peaks team: Rick Melchin, Robin Auld and John Bachynski. Our team spends the majority of its time in Bearspaw, with our office on-site at our Willow Creek development. John Bachynski and his family also live in Bearspaw and are proud to be active community members.

Ten Peaks' focus in Bearspaw is evident in its residential estate community, Willow Creek at Bearspaw. The team went through a 3-year process to gain approvals for Willow Creek where new show homes and custom residences are currently being built. Willow Creek is a quarter section property where the bulk of the land topography was maintained. It features 19 acres of waterscapes and a $2M+ investment in engineering and waterscape design helped to resolve storm water issues for nearly 800 acres in the area. Until the excavation was complete, over 20 million gallons of water was pumped to the Willow Creek site annually to relieve flooding in neighbouring properties. The waterworks were designed with the help of Ducks Unlimited and an environmental consultant to provide working, living habitats for avian wildlife. The design also acts as a bio-filter for run-off from lots both on and off site.

Our commitment to the environment runs deep as our team has been leaders in environmental and wildlife stewardship for development within Canada. Rick Melchin was responsible for purchasing, envisioning and developing the Three Sisters project in Canmore. Rick and his team were the first to introduce wildlife corridors in their development (including the unique wildlife underpasses along the TransCanada Hwy). It is fair to say that environmental and wildlife issues are part of our corporate character. That is why the landscape and both preservation and introduction of trees, vegetation and wildlife are important to the structure of what Ten Peak's produces in Bearspaw. We believe our philosophy fits with what we understand Bearspaw to be about.
